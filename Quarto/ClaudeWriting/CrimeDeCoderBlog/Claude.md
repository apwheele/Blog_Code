# CrimeDeCoderBlog/CLAUDE.md

## Project Overview
Markdown files for Crime Decoder's consulting website, including blog posts, service descriptions, and educational content. Target audience includes police departments, attorneys, non-profits, and cyber security professionals.

## Writing Style
- **Professional but approachable**: Balance expertise with readability
- **Client-focused**: Emphasize practical value and outcomes
- **Clear explanations**: Make technical forensics/data concepts accessible to non-technical readers
- **Authority without jargon**: Demonstrate expertise while avoiding unnecessary technical terms
- **Solution-oriented**: Frame content around client needs and problem-solving

## Content Types

### Blog Posts
- Thought leadership on digital forensics, data analysis, and civil litigation
- Case studies (anonymized, focusing on methodology)
- Industry trends and emerging issues
- Educational content for legal professionals
- Length: 800-1500 words typically

### Service Pages
- Clear value propositions
- Capabilities and methodologies
- Target client profiles
- Call-to-action focused
- Length: 400-800 words typically

## Tone Guidelines
- Confident but not arrogant
- Educational but not condescending
- Technical precision without overwhelming detail
- Empathetic to client challenges
- Evidence-based and credible

## SEO Considerations
- Include relevant keywords naturally
- Suggest meta descriptions
- Use clear headings (H2, H3) for structure
- Internal linking opportunities to other services/posts
- Alt text suggestions for images

## Key Guidelines
- Less technical depth than book writing, more application-focused
- Real-world examples trump abstract explanations
- Maintain ethical boundaries in discussing case work
- Position services within broader litigation/investigation context
- Proofread for legal industry terminology accuracy
