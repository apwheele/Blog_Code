## Workload Analysis Services

Workload analysis for police departments can span different areas, such as:

 - Do you have the right number of officers to effectively respond to calls for service?
 - Are you spatially allocating officers to minimize response times?
 - Are you effectively triaging detective case assignments?

These questions often mix advanced statistical analysis, [discrete event simulations](https://andrewpwheeler.com/2021/06/01/using-simulations-to-show-roi-for-predictive-models-in-python/), and optimization models (e.g. linear programming), to develop strategies, policies, or empirically justified forecasts to better meet the demands for your police department. The **CRIME** De-Coder has extensive experience not only in the number crunching part, but critically understanding how to translate real demands and constraints that police departments face into tangible plans to address your local crime and safety issues.
 
### Redistricting

Response times are critically important for public safety -- faster response times by police and emergency services can make the difference between life and death (Circo & Wheeler, 2021). The **CRIME** De-Coder has written custom applications to reduce drive responses to calls for service, as well as to rebalance workloads via redistricting (Wheeler, 2018).

![](./images/Redistrict_TwelveAreas.png)

This solution resulted in an estimated reduction of over 20% in drive times for Carrollton PD, as well as balanced workloads for individual patrol areas.

### Implementing Gun Violence Reduction Initiatives

The **CRIME** De-Coder in his career has worked with multiple police departments implementing gun violence reduction initiatives. These include programs like [Focused Deterrence](https://www.rand.org/pubs/tools/TL261/better-policing-toolkit/all-strategies/focused-deterrence/in-depth.html), or [chronic offender initiatives](https://www.ojp.gov/ncjrs/virtual-library/abstracts/pulling-levers-chronic-offenders-high-crime-settings-and-theory).

These involve not just seeing if the program results in subsequent crime reductions (aka policy analysis), but also to use effective metrics to identify those criminal groups to begin with (via [social network analysis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_network_analysis_(criminology)) and [predictive models](ProgramAnalysis.html)), but also to methods to prioritize who to target in such interventions. The **CRIME** De-Coder has developed custom social network analysis methods (Wheeler et al., 2019), and also has experience in [linear programming](https://andrewpwheeler.com/2019/09/27/optimal-treatment-assignment-with-network-spillovers/) to identify optimal allocation models.

### Prioritizing Detective Case Loads

Optimizing workloads is not all about responding to calls for service. Effectively allocating detective resources can result in much better case clearance rates and the ability to [investigate more cases](https://andrewpwheeler.com/2019/11/15/knowing-when-to-fold-them-a-quantitative-approach-to-ending-investigations/). Predictive models to triage detective cases typically involve incorporating multiple factors, such as:

 - the probability a case will be resolved
 - the severity of the case
 - the probability of subsequent follow up crimes (Wheeler et al., 2021)

It is the case that most police departments in detective case assignments implicitly take these factors into account, but empirical methods can make that task less ad-hoc and less likely to miss serial offenders that may be contributing to regular crimes.
