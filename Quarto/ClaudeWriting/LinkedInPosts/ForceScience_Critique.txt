For a few notes:

The scales Ian used are not made up from AI, they are basic ways to tell if research meets certain minimal standards, like having a control group or identifying a specific outcome you are measuring. And have been around for decades. Force Science not understanding this is somewhat beyond the pale. (For a hint as to what a reasonable response would be, it would say "yeah we think these LEEF publications are important summaries, but here are the actual citations that formed those opinions". It is not asking the world.)

Placing the burden on others to "disprove" unvalidated findings can result in obviously absurd scenarios. Imagine I said "officers who are abducted by aliens make better decisions". You say that is ridiculous, and I say in response "you don't have any evidence that disproves that". It is pretty silly to say in response to the critique "you aren't using actual validated scientific evidence to form your opinions" that "you can't prove my opinions are wrong".

Resorting to impugning the character of others (saying Ian is just doing it for civil litigation), and saying my work is good because I have good qualifications (appealing to authority), are common bad argument tactics.

You can personally think that the Force Science training is good, and their opinions in civil litigation is right. That does not make it reasonable though for them to continue under false pretenses that the work they are doing is backed by science. Much of it appears to clearly not be.
